Introduction to Computers

 

Computer is an electronic device that performs processes, calculations, and operations based on instructions provided by a software or hardware program. It has the ability to accept data (input), process it, and produce outputs. Computers can also store data for later uses in appropriate storage devices, and retrieve where it is necessary. Nowadays, computers are primarily used for a variety of purposes ranging from browsing the web, writing documents, editing videos, creating applications, playing video games, etc.

Computer System

are all of their components, software, hardware, and peripherals. It is programmed to perform a certain task as per computer program instructions.

Hardware VS. Software

Hardware

Hardware are any part of your computer that has a physical structure, such as the keyboard or mouse. This also includes all of the computer’s internal parts.

Software

Software are a set of instructions that tells the hardware what to do and how to do it. Examples of software include web browsers, games, and word processors.

Computers rely on both of these in order to function.
A quick example is using a web browser to search on the internet
while using a mouse to navigate from page to page.

COMPUTER COMPONENTS

Input Devices

Input devices are pieces of computer hardware that are connected to a computer that sends information into the computer used for interaction and control.


Examples include, but not limited to:

Mouse

Keyboard

Webcam

Stylus

Microphone

Output Devices

Any device or piece of computer hardware equipment that converts information into human-readable form. These are in the form of text, graphics, tactile, audio, or video.


Examples include, but not limited to:

Monitor

Headphones

Speaker

Printer

Projector

CPU

Often called the brain of a computer, the CPU is a piece of hardware that carries out the instruction of a computer program. It receives instructions represented as a series of numbers, decodes these instructions into signals, and finally, executes these instructions by sending these signals to relevant parts of the CPU to be completed.

Modern CPU sockets and processor packages are built in following architectures:

  • Pin Grid Array (PGA) - the pins are on the underside of the processor package and are inserted into the motherboard CPU socket.
  • Land Grid Array (LGA) - the pins are in the socket instead of on the processor

Memory

Computer memory refers to the device used to store data or programs on a temporary or permanent basis for immediate use in computer or related computer hardware and digital electronic devices.

There are 2 types of Computer Memory:

  • RAM (Random Access Memory) - temporary working storage for data and programs that are being accessed by CPU.
  • ROM (Read Only Memory) - non-volatile memory that permanently stores instruction from your computer.

Storage

Storage Devices are used for storing non-volatile data in a computer system. The operating system is also located in the storage device, so the faster the storage device, the quicker the operating system can run. Hard Disk Drives, Solid State Drives, and Optical storage devices are some examples of storage devices.

Let's take a look at the two most common storage device found in computers.

HDD

Hard Disk Drives are one of the most common types of storage since it is relatively cheap. HDD is reliable, and it can store a vast amount of data. These storage devices contain moving parts that work using electromagnets to read and write data. Reading and writing speed is slower than SSD because of physically moving parts. The physical mechanisms also make noise, and it can easily break, which can corrupt the data stored.

SSD

Solid State Drives are also a common storage device; however, it is much more expensive. This storage device utilizes flash-based memory, which is significantly faster than HDD. The physical dimensions of the SSD are smaller. This storage is more durable than HDD because it does not require moving physical parts.

Case

Computer case is usually referred to as the system case. It is also referred to as a box, chassis or main unit. It is essentially a metal box that contains the other parts of the computer, like the motherboard, hard drive, optical drive, floppy disk drive, etc.

There are 4 different types of computer case.

Full Tower

is the largest of the four cases. It is used for servers and some high-end workstations. It gives extra room for storage, power, and adapter cards.

Mid-Tower

a computer case that allows the use of multiple drives and almost any type of motherboard with an acceptable overall size.

Mini Tower

used for high-end users and power users. It has extra space for devices like video cards, extra adapter cards, and storage devices.

Small Form Factor

is the smallest out of the standard cases. It has limited expansion ability.

Power Supply

Computer systems need the power to run. The PSU converts AC power into lower voltage DC power and runs it through the different components of a computer. Powerful computer components like high-end GPUs require high power. The better the power supply, the more watts it can supply to computer components to run smoothly. The desktop computer power supply form factors include:

Advanced Technology (AT)

Original power supply for legacy computer systems

AT Extended (ATX)

the updated version of the AT

ATX12V

the most common power supply on the market today

EPS12V

originally designed for network servers but is now commonly used in high-end desktop models.

Connectors & Ports

A port is a connector at the back or side of a computer where you plug in an external device such as a printer, keyboard, scanner, mouse, or modem. This connection allows instructions and data to flow between the computer and the device. These computer ports are also commonly referred to as the Input/Output ports (I/O ports).

Below are common examples

USB

(Universal Serial Bus)

this port is very versatile in use; It can be used for various purposes, such as to transfer data, to connect peripheral devices, and even as an interface for charging devices such as smartphones, digital cameras, etc. Today, it has replaced PS/2 connectors, game ports, serial and parallel ports, etc.

3.5 mm Audio Jack

It is a small round connector, port, or an audio jack commonly found on laptops, computers, phones, etc. It is designed to connect to wired headphones and speakers. In other words, it accepts a pin-shaped plug from a headphone, earphone, etc. The measurement "3.5 mm" denotes the diameter of the connector.

HDMI

(High Definition Media Interface)

is a digital interface developed to connect high definition devices such as digital cameras, gaming consoles, etc., to computers and TVs with HDMI ports. Besides this, it can carry uncompressed video and uncompressed or compressed audio signals. The advanced version of HDMI, such as 2.0, can transfer video signals of up to a resolution of 4096x2160.

VGA port

(Video Graphics Array)

This port is commonly found in computers, projectors, and high definition TVs. It is a D-sub connector called DR-15 as it has 15 pins, which are arranged in 3 rows with five pins in each row. It was most often used to connect CPUs with CRT monitors. Still, most of the LCD and LED monitors come with VGA ports. However, these ports don't assure high picture quality as VGA can carry only analogue video signals up to a resolution of 648X480.


and many more! like: DVI, Display Port, Network, Thunderbolt 1 2 & 3, PS/2

MOTHERBOARD

The motherboard is also called the system board or main board. This is the most important part of a computer. It is medium communication for the entire computer system. All computer components or devices are connected to the motherboard.

It facilitates different slots and ports, in which different parts of the computer can connect in order to run. Some examples are CPU slots or sockets in which is used to connect the microprocessor to the motherboard, RAM slots for attaching RAM chips, and PCI slots (Peripheral Component Interface) that allows insertion of expansion cards like network cards, sound cards, and video cards.

Adapter Cards

Adapter cards increase a computer's functionality by adding controllers for specific devices or by replacing malfunctioning ports.

NIC

Original power supply for legacy computer systems.

Wireless NIC

connects a computer to a network using a network cable.

Sound adapter

provides audio capability.

Video adapter

provides graphic capability.

Modem adapter

connects a computer to the Internet using a phone line.

SCSI adapter

connects SCSI devices, such as hard drives or tape drives, to a computer.

RAID adapter

connects multiple hard drives to a computer to provide redundancy and to improve performance.
